Welcome to The Bookshelf!

Hi, I’m Chris. I love books.

I was going to say I love reading books, but I own far too many that I haven’t yet read, so maybe the first statement is more accurate.

I’ve been keeping track of my reading through various means since 2007. At various times I’ve used my blog, Goodreads, and iOS apps, and never been 100% happy with any of them. I’m sure I won’t be 100% happy with this site, either, but it’s my favorite so far!

Dig around here and you can find

  • the books I’ve read,
  • which books I want to read,
  • useless statistics

If you’re really interested in what I’m reading, you can subscribe to the feed (feed with only reviews here).

Thanks for the inspiration (and a lot of the code!) for this site go to Rixx.

Here are a few of the books I’ve read recently:

Making It So

by Patrick Stewart · published 2023 · read 2023-12-06
★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Sir Patrick's memoir is just as delightful as you would hope. From stories about his humble beginnings in Yorkshire to tales about making ST:TNG, Stewart's tone is engaging. I don't typically do audiobooks but I would go listen to this one simply to hear the book in his own voice. A truly lovely book.

Titanium Noir

by Nick Harkaway · published 2023 · read 2023-11-26
★ ★ ★ ★ ☆
Cyberpunk hard boiled noir detective story. Entertaining stuff.

The New Being

by Paul Tillich · published 2005 · read 2023-11-23
★ ★ ★ ★ ★
A short collection of short sermons, a few of which are truly brilliant. "What is truth?" is one I need to revisit sometime very soon.

Girl One

by Sara Flannery Murphy · published 2022 · read 2023-11-19
★ ★ ★ ★ ☆
A little bit sci-fi, a little bit adventure thriller. An entertaining easy read.

The Talmud

by Barry Scott Wimpfheimer · published 2020 · read 2023-11-16
★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆
I've enjoyed a lot of the Biographies of Ancient Books series but this one left me flat.
